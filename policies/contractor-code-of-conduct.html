How we work

Contractor Code of Conduct

Applies to: subcontractors engaged by Fifth Castle Media  ·  Version 1.0  ·  Last updated 17 June 2026
Five Castles Pty Ltd (ABN 76 651 385 545), trading as Fifth Castle Media

What this is

Fifth Castle Media engages skilled independent contractors to deliver work for our clients. This Code sets out the conditions every contractor agrees to as part of being engaged. It sits alongside your written subcontractor agreement, which remains the binding contract for any specific job; this Code does not replace it.

You remain an independent contractor. You run your own business, control how you carry out the work, supply your own primary equipment, and are free to accept or decline any job we offer. Meeting the standards below is part of working professionally for a client, and it does not change that independence.

Before and during an engagement, you agree that you will

Hold your own insurance and credentials

  • Maintain your own current public liability insurance, and provide a certificate of currency on request.
  • Hold any licences, accreditations and registrations your work requires, and keep them current.
  • For drone work, meet the requirements in the Drone and RPAS Safety Policy, including your own aviation public liability cover and a valid remote pilot licence. No certificate, no flight.

Work safely

  • Follow the Work Health and Safety Policy and any client site rules, and complete site inductions where required.
  • Provide a safe work method statement for higher-risk work (such as working at height, rigging, electrical work or drone operations) where we or the client require one.

Conduct yourself professionally

  • Uphold the Code of Conduct, the Equal Opportunity, Anti-Discrimination and Bullying Policy and the Sexual Harassment Prevention Policy and Plan, including the protocol for fitting microphones.
  • Represent Fifth Castle Media well on client sites.

Protect client information and footage

  • Keep client information confidential, and handle, transfer and delete footage according to the Confidentiality and Footage Handling Policy and the Information Security and BYOD Policy.
  • Assign intellectual property in your deliverables to us as set out in your agreement.
  • Do not post or share client work without our written permission, in line with the Social Media Policy.

Work with children safely

  • On any job involving children, hold a current Working with Children Check and follow the Child Safety and Wellbeing Policy.

Invoice professionally

  • Quote and invoice for the agreed work, using your own ABN, and apply GST where you are registered.
  • Provide your invoice promptly after a job so we can pay on the agreed terms.

If something is not right

If you have a safety concern, a conduct concern, or any other issue on a job, raise it with Arek straight away, or use the Grievance and Complaints Policy. We would always rather hear about a problem early.

If this Code is breached

Serious or repeated breaches may mean we do not offer further work, and may end a current engagement under the terms of your agreement.

Review

This Code is reviewed at least once a year, and sooner if our work or the law changes.
