Privacy Policy

Applies to: clients, talent, employees and subcontractors  ·  Version 1.0  ·  Last updated 17 June 2026
Five Castles Pty Ltd (ABN 76 651 385 545), trading as Fifth Castle Media

Our commitment

Fifth Castle Media respects the privacy of the people we work with. As a small business, we are not bound by the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth), but we choose to follow its principles, because handling people's information carefully is part of being trusted in their workplaces and in front of their cameras.

What we collect

  • Client and contact details, such as names, business details, email addresses and phone numbers.
  • Talent and participant information, including appearance releases and consent forms.
  • Footage and photographs of identifiable people, captured as part of a job.
  • Crew and contractor details needed to engage and pay people.

How we use it

We use this information only to deliver the work we have been engaged for, to run and manage the business, and to pay the people who work with us. We do not sell personal information.

How we store and protect it

Information is stored securely and access is limited to people who need it. Devices and accounts are protected in line with our Information Security and BYOD Policy. Raw footage is retained for a set period after a project, after which it is deleted, as set out in the Confidentiality and Footage Handling Policy and the client agreement.

Who we share it with

  • Subcontractors who need it to do a job, and who are bound to keep it confidential.
  • Software and cloud providers we use to run the business, store files and process payments.

We share only what is needed, and we do not disclose personal information for any unrelated purpose without consent, unless the law requires it.

Your rights

You can ask what personal information we hold about you, ask us to correct it, or ask us to delete footage and information where we are able to. Contact Arek Rainczuk at info@fivecastles.com.au.

Concerns

If you have a privacy concern, contact us first and we will work to resolve it. You can also contact the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner at oaic.gov.au or on 1300 363 992.

Review

This policy is reviewed at least once a year, and sooner if our work or the law changes.
