Working with children

Child Safety and Wellbeing Policy

Applies to: any job where we film around children  ·  Version 1.0  ·  Last updated 17 June 2026
Five Castles Pty Ltd (ABN 76 651 385 545), trading as Fifth Castle Media

Our commitment

Some of our work takes us into schools, community organisations and events where children are present. Fifth Castle Media is committed to keeping children safe and treating them with respect. We have zero tolerance for child abuse of any kind.

When this policy applies

This policy applies on any job where children are present or being filmed. On those jobs, it is required reading for everyone on the crew. Because our contact with children is occasional rather than ongoing, the rules below are written for that setting, and they sit alongside the child safety requirements of the school, organisation or event hosting us.

Working with Children Checks

Anyone working on a job that involves children must hold a current Working with Children Check. We confirm this for each such job before it goes ahead, and contractors must provide evidence of theirs as a condition of engagement.

How we conduct ourselves around children

  • Never be alone, one-on-one, with a child. Stay where other adults can see you.
  • Follow the host organisation's child safety rules and the direction of their staff at all times.
  • Treat children with respect and patience, and keep all contact appropriate and professional.
  • Only film children where the right consent is in place, arranged through the host organisation and the child's parent or guardian.
  • Handle any images of children carefully, in line with the Privacy Policy and the Confidentiality and Footage Handling Policy.

If you have a concern about a child's safety

Act on it

If a child is in immediate danger, call 000. Otherwise, report any concern about a child's safety to the host organisation's child safety contact and to Arek as soon as possible. Serious concerns, including any concern about abuse, are reported to Victoria Police or the relevant child protection authority. Never keep a concern about a child to yourself.

Review

This policy is reviewed at least once a year, and sooner if our work or the law changes.
