Information Security and BYOD Policy

Applies to: employees and subcontractors  ·  Version 1.0  ·  Last updated 17 June 2026
Five Castles Pty Ltd (ABN 76 651 385 545), trading as Fifth Castle Media

Why this matters

We work on our own laptops, cameras and phones, which is normal for a small production business. That makes each of us responsible for keeping client material and personal information secure on the devices and accounts we use. This policy sets the baseline. "BYOD" means "bring your own device", the gear you own and use for work.

Securing your devices

  • Lock every device with a strong passcode, PIN or biometric, and set it to lock automatically.
  • Keep operating systems and software up to date.
  • Turn on device encryption where it is available, and use reputable security software where relevant.
  • Do not leave devices or memory cards unattended on a job or in a vehicle.

Accounts and passwords

  • Use a strong, unique password for each work-related account, kept in a password manager.
  • Turn on multi-factor authentication on important accounts, such as email and cloud storage.
  • Do not share logins, and do not reuse personal passwords for work accounts.

Storing client material

  • Keep client footage and files in the approved storage we use for a job, not on random personal services.
  • Only access what you need for the work in front of you.
  • Handle and delete footage in line with the Confidentiality and Footage Handling Policy.

Lost or stolen devices

Report it immediately

If a device, phone, laptop or memory card holding work data is lost or stolen, tell Arek straight away so access can be revoked and the client told if needed. Acting fast is what limits the damage.

Ending an engagement

When a job or engagement ends, return or securely delete any client material you hold, and confirm you have done so.

Review

This policy is reviewed at least once a year, and sooner if our work or the law changes.
