Confidentiality and Footage Handling Policy

Applies to: employees and subcontractors  ·  Version 1.0  ·  Last updated 17 June 2026
Five Castles Pty Ltd (ABN 76 651 385 545), trading as Fifth Castle Media

The principle

Clients let us into their workplaces and trust us with footage of their people and their projects. That material is theirs and it is confidential. Looking after it properly, from capture through to deletion, is one of the most important things we do.

Confidential information

Anything you learn about a client's business, people or plans through a job is confidential. Do not discuss it outside the work, and do not use it for any purpose other than delivering the job.

Who owns the footage

Ownership of footage and finished deliverables is set out in the client agreement. As a rule, Fifth Castle Media retains copyright in what it produces and grants the client a licence to use it once the work is paid for. Footage is captured on behalf of Fifth Castle Media, and a contractor does not retain any right to keep or reuse it.

Handling footage

  • Store it securely. Keep footage on protected drives and accounts, in line with the Information Security and BYOD Policy.
  • Transfer it securely. Hand over footage to us through the agreed method, and confirm it has been received before deleting your copy.
  • Keep it organised. Label and store project files so the right material can be found and the right material can be deleted.

Deleting footage from your own devices

For subcontractors

Once you have handed over a job's footage and we have confirmed it, delete it from your own cameras, cards, drives and accounts. Do not keep a personal copy, and do not reuse client footage in your own portfolio or showreel without our written permission.

Retention and deletion

We retain raw footage for the period set out in the client agreement (currently 12 months from delivery, unless agreed otherwise), so we can meet reasonable client requests. After that, raw footage may be deleted. We will also delete material sooner where a client asks and we are able to.

Using footage in our own marketing

We may show finished work in our portfolio and marketing unless a client has asked us in writing not to. Sharing client work on social media follows the Social Media Policy, which requires the client's permission.

If something goes wrong

If footage or confidential information is lost, accessed by the wrong person, or shared by mistake, tell Arek immediately so we can contain it and tell the client where needed.

Review

This policy is reviewed at least once a year, and sooner if our work or the law changes.
