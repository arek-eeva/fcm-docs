How we work

Code of Conduct

Applies to: employees and subcontractors  ·  Version 1.0  ·  Last updated 17 June 2026
Five Castles Pty Ltd (ABN 76 651 385 545), trading as Fifth Castle Media

Why this matters

When you are on a job, you represent Fifth Castle Media. Clients trust us in their workplaces, around their people, and often on the most important day of a project. How we conduct ourselves is the brand. This Code sets the standard we all hold to.

On every job

  • Be professional and prepared. Turn up on time, ready, and knowing the brief.
  • Be respectful. Treat clients, talent, crew and the public with courtesy and patience. People in front of a camera are often nervous. Put them at ease.
  • Follow the site rules. On client and construction sites, complete any induction and follow their rules in addition to ours.
  • Work safely. Follow the Work Health and Safety Policy. If something is not safe, stop and raise it.
  • Look after the space. Leave a location as you found it. Manage cables and gear so the site stays tidy and safe.

Respect and fair treatment

Discrimination, bullying and harassment have no place on our jobs. This is set out in the Equal Opportunity, Anti-Discrimination and Bullying Policy and the Sexual Harassment Prevention Policy and Plan, which everyone is expected to follow.

Fitness for work

Come to work fit to do it safely. Do not work while affected by alcohol or drugs, including anything that impairs your ability to drive, rig lighting, work at height, or operate a drone. If a prescription medicine could affect your work, let us know so we can plan around it.

Honesty and conflicts of interest

Be honest in how you represent the business, quote your work, and record your hours. If you have an interest that could conflict with a client's or ours, tell us so we can deal with it openly.

Confidentiality

Client information and footage are confidential. Handle them according to the Confidentiality and Footage Handling Policy and the Social Media Policy. In short: what happens on a client's job stays with the client unless they have agreed otherwise.

Speaking up

If you have a concern about conduct, safety or anything else, raise it. The Grievance and Complaints Policy sets out how. No one is penalised for raising a concern in good faith.

If the Code is breached

Breaches are taken seriously and handled fairly. Depending on what happened, the response can range from a conversation through to ending an engagement or employment, and reporting to the authorities where the law requires it.

Review

This Code is reviewed at least once a year, and sooner if our work or the law changes.
