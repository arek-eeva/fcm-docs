Equal Opportunity, Anti-Discrimination and Bullying Policy

Applies to: employees and subcontractors  ·  Version 1.0  ·  Last updated 17 June 2026
Five Castles Pty Ltd (ABN 76 651 385 545), trading as Fifth Castle Media

Our commitment

Fifth Castle Media treats people fairly. Everyone on our jobs deserves to be judged on their work and treated with respect, free from discrimination, bullying and harassment. This is the right way to work, and it is required by the Equal Opportunity Act 2010 (Vic), federal anti-discrimination law, and the Fair Work Act 2009 (Cth).

Who this applies to

This policy applies to employees and subcontractors, and to how we treat clients, talent and members of the public. It covers our own space, client sites, location work, travel and work-related social settings.

Discrimination

Discrimination is treating someone unfairly, or applying a rule that disadvantages them, because of a personal characteristic protected by law. Protected characteristics include age, disability, race, sex, gender identity, sexual orientation, religious or political belief, pregnancy or breastfeeding, marital or relationship status, parental or carer responsibilities, physical features, and association with someone who has one of these characteristics.

We do not discriminate in who we hire, engage or work with, or in how we treat people during a job.

Bullying

Bullying is repeated unreasonable behaviour towards a person or group that creates a risk to health and safety. It can include intimidation, humiliation, persistent unfair criticism, or excluding someone. Reasonable, fairly delivered direction about work, such as feedback or allocating tasks, is not bullying.

Sexual harassment

Sexual harassment is a form of unlawful conduct that we manage separately and in detail. See the Sexual Harassment Prevention Policy and Plan.

What we expect

  • Treat everyone on a job with respect, including people you have only just met.
  • Do not make decisions, jokes or comments based on someone's protected characteristics.
  • Do not engage in or go along with bullying or harassment.
  • If you see it happen, do not ignore it. Raise it.

How to raise a concern

Speak to Arek Rainczuk at info@fivecastles.com.au or on 0467 676 205. You can also raise it through the Grievance and Complaints process.

You can go to an external body instead of, or as well as, raising it with us, which is the right path if a concern involves Arek:

  • Fair Work Commission (can make orders to stop bullying): fwc.gov.au
  • Victorian Equal Opportunity and Human Rights Commission: humanrights.vic.gov.au, 1300 292 153
  • Australian Human Rights Commission: humanrights.gov.au, 1300 656 419
  • WorkSafe Victoria (bullying as a work health and safety matter): 13 23 60

No victimisation

No one will be disadvantaged for raising a concern in good faith or supporting someone who has. Victimising a person for doing so is itself a breach of this policy and the law.

Review

This policy is reviewed at least once a year, and sooner if our work or the law changes.
