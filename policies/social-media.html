Social Media Policy

Applies to: employees and subcontractors  ·  Version 1.0  ·  Last updated 17 June 2026
Five Castles Pty Ltd (ABN 76 651 385 545), trading as Fifth Castle Media

Why this matters

We make content for a living, and behind-the-scenes glimpses are part of how creative businesses are seen. That is fine, with one firm rule: a client's work is theirs, and it is not ours to share until they have agreed.

Protecting client confidentiality

  • Do not post client footage, images, names, logos or project details without the client's written permission.
  • Behind-the-scenes content from a job needs the client's and any identifiable person's consent before it goes up.
  • If you are unsure whether something can be shared, ask first. Once it is posted, it is hard to take back.

Representing the business

When you post about Fifth Castle Media, or are identifiable as working with us, what you say reflects on the brand. Be respectful and professional. Do not post anything that would breach the standards in the Equal Opportunity, Anti-Discrimination and Bullying Policy or the Sexual Harassment Prevention Policy and Plan.

Personal accounts

Your personal accounts are your own, and your personal views are yours to hold. Just do not present them as the views of Fifth Castle Media, and keep client confidentiality even in personal posts.

Official Fifth Castle Media accounts

Only people authorised by Arek post on the official Fifth Castle Media accounts. If you would like something featured there, send it to us and we will handle it.

If something goes wrong online

If you post something you should not have, or you see content that breaches this policy or risks a client's confidentiality, tell Arek as soon as possible so it can be dealt with.

Review

This policy is reviewed at least once a year, and sooner if our work or the law changes.
