Drone and RPAS Safety Policy

Applies to: drone operators engaged by Fifth Castle Media  ·  Version 1.0  ·  Last updated 17 June 2026
Five Castles Pty Ltd (ABN 76 651 385 545), trading as Fifth Castle Media

Our position

When we fly a drone for a client, we are flying commercially, and that comes with rules set by the Civil Aviation Safety Authority (CASA). Safety and the law come first, every time, ahead of any shot. A drone, or remotely piloted aircraft (RPAS), is only flown by a properly credentialed and insured operator under conditions we have checked.

What every drone operator must hold

No certificate, no flight

Before flying on a Fifth Castle Media job, the operator must hold, and provide evidence of:

1. Their own current aviation and RPAS public liability insurance.
2. A valid CASA remote pilot licence or the accreditation required for the operation, plus any operator authorisation the flight requires.
3. A drone registered with CASA.

Why operators carry their own insurance

Fifth Castle Media's own public liability and professional indemnity insurance do not cover drone operation. For that reason, every drone operator we engage carries their own aviation cover and provides a certificate of currency before any flight. This is a firm condition of engagement.

The pre-flight check we run

Before a shoot date involving drone footage is locked in, we check the airspace and the conditions for the specific location, and give the client a clear yes or no on whether the flight can go ahead as planned. This check is a guide for planning. It is never permission to fly.

On the day

The licensed operator is the pilot in command and is responsible for confirming that the flight is legal and safe before taking off, using CASA's official information sources. The operator flies within the rules for the operation, which generally means:

  • Keeping a safe distance from people and not flying over people who are not part of the operation.
  • Flying in daylight and within visual line of sight, unless properly authorised otherwise.
  • Staying clear of controlled and restricted airspace, and other aircraft.
  • Stopping the flight if conditions, people or aircraft make it unsafe.

Review

This policy is reviewed at least once a year, and sooner if our work or the aviation rules change. Our internal procedure for running the per-job check is held separately and kept current with CASA's requirements.
