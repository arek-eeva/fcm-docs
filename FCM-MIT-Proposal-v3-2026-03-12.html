Our Understanding

The campaign in context

MIT is one of only 17 of 161 Australian higher education institutions with self-accrediting authority. It delivers practical, industry-integrated courses across business and technology at city-centre campuses in Melbourne and Sydney, serving a predominantly international student community.

Two changes are reshaping the enrolment environment. Tighter immigration policy has reduced the pipeline of new international arrivals. And from April 2026, legislative change will prohibit the agent-led recruitment of switching students, a channel that has historically delivered transfers to many institutions. For MIT, this creates an opening. Students already in Australia, already enrolled elsewhere, can now be reached directly through digital channels. MIT's new scholarship offer gives them a clear and credible reason to act.

MIT's own words
"Take part. Take charge. Own tomorrow."
The institution

Self-accrediting. 84.6% postgraduate satisfaction vs 80.3% national average. Practical, industry-based learning with smaller classes and stronger individual support.

The audience

Onshore international students currently enrolled elsewhere. Cost-conscious, time-pressed, and open to switching if the case is clear, trustworthy, and easy to act on.

The message

MIT offers accredited, practical education in a genuinely supportive environment, with a scholarship available now for eligible students considering a switch.

The constraint

Direct price comparison is off the table. The tone must inspire trust and agency without sounding promotional. "Take part. Take charge." not "we're cheaper."

Creative Direction

Four treatments. One shoot day.

Rather than four versions of the same idea, we recommend four distinct treatments. Each uses overlapping footage from a single half-day shoot, keeping production costs contained while giving the campaign real variety to test. Final scripts and concepts will come from Sara Tiefenbrun. These are our production frameworks.

01
The Switcher

First-person story

A current MIT student who transferred from another institution tells their story directly to camera. Teleprompter-guided for clean, confident delivery. Filmed in a relaxed outdoor campus setting. B-roll of student life layers over the narration. Personal, specific, and built on real outcomes: smaller classes, accessible support, renewed confidence. Ends with an animated call to action linking directly to the scholarship landing page.

02
The Counsellor

The institution speaks

A member of MIT's student support team, filmed in the office environment. A student is seen entering the frame at the open, establishing context. The support person introduces the scholarship offer and explains who it is for. Credible, warm, and institutional. Suited to an audience who already knows MIT but needs reassurance and a clear next step. The message stays factual: what is available, who qualifies, how to find out more.

03
The Campus Voice

Direct and energetic

Filmed in a lecture or tutorial space. A student speaks directly to camera with energy and confidence. Shorter lines, faster pacing. Highlights the hands-on, collaborative side of MIT's teaching approach. Designed to feel immediate rather than testimonial. Good for testing against the warmer storytelling formats, particularly for younger audiences or those who respond to a more direct call to action.

04
The Announcement

Copy-led, graphic-driven

A clean, image-led format. Scripted voiceover paired with B-roll of campus life, lecture spaces, and Melbourne CBD exterior. Bold text on screen carries the core message. No single on-camera narrator. Precise and campaign-focused. Well-suited to a shorter 15-second cut, or as a variant for audiences who engage more with visual information than personal stories. Also the lowest-friction option for MIT if sourcing willing student talent proves difficult.
