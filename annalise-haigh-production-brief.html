Project Overview

This shoot tells Annalise Haigh's story. It connects two key areas of DSA's work: leadership development and employment. Annalise was a recipient of the Sherry Hogan Leadership Scholarship at the 2024 World Down Syndrome Congress, where she grew from being reluctant to speak publicly to delivering a confident, unscripted speech on stage. She now works at Speak Out, a disability advocacy organisation in Hobart, and lives independently.

From this single shoot, two videos will be produced. Both share much of the same footage, with edits tailored to suit each audience. Existing footage of Annalise at the 2024 Congress will be woven into the leadership edit to show her growth over time.

Video 1 — Leadership Impact

Audience: Sherry Hogan Foundation and other funders.

Goal: Show the life-changing impact of leadership training. Funders see real-world results and choose to continue or expand their investment.

Focus: Confidence growth, independence, willingness to take on new challenges, the link from leadership skills to meaningful employment.

Video 2 — Employment Good News Story

Audience: Employers, families, and the wider community.

Goal: Employers take the next step and explore inclusive hiring. Families and individuals feel informed and encouraged.

Focus: The real work Annalise does, her strengths, the benefits of inclusive employment for the workplace, how meaningful work builds independence and purpose.

Interview Setup Direction

We want three visually distinct interview setups so each interviewee has a different look on screen. If only one space is available, adjust the camera angle, background, and lighting direction between each interview. If multiple rooms or areas are available, consider quick setups in different spaces. The goal is that when the three interviews are intercut, each person is clearly in their own visual world.

Lighting Direction

  • Cinematic portrait lighting for all three interviews. This is the priority. The interviews carry the story.
  • Key light slightly off-centre with soft fill. Look for depth and dimension in the face.
  • Use the available space creatively: window light as key or rim, supplemented with LED if needed.
  • Background separation is important. Use depth of field and practical lights in the background where possible.
  • Each setup should have a different feel: consider varying the lighting direction (key left vs key right) or background tone between subjects.

Framing Direction

  • Setup 1 (Annalise): Slightly tighter framing. She is the heart of the story. Capture warmth and personality.
  • Setup 2 (Mark): Shift angle. Consider a workplace element in the background (desk, shelves, office context). Employer perspective.
  • Setup 3 (Liz): Shift angle again. Softer background if possible. She provides the reflective, narrative voice.
  • All interviews framed as a standard off-axis interview (subject looking just past camera to interviewer).

B-Roll Shot List

Key rules for all b-roll:
1. Shoot everything at 50fps for smooth slow-motion in post.
2. Introduce slow, deliberate camera movement throughout. No static locked-off b-roll. Gentle slides, pushes, or pulls on a slider or gimbal. Even handheld with controlled breathing works if it is smooth.
3. For every b-roll scenario, capture at least two different angles: a wide and a mid, or a mid and a close-up. This gives the editor options and makes cuts feel natural.
4. Watch for moments that echo what was said in the interviews. If Annalise mentions something specific, try to capture b-roll that supports it.

Technical Settings

Camera

  • 4K, Rec.709 colour profile (no log). Log requires perfect lighting and significantly longer grading time — not worth the trade-off for this shoot.
  • Manual white balance at every location. Do not rely on auto white balance. When mixing artificial lighting with ambient light, match the colour temperature so footage cuts together cleanly in the edit. Re-set white balance when moving between locations.
  • 25fps for all interview/presenter shots
  • 50fps for all b-roll (to allow for slow-motion in the edit)
  • Shutter: 1/50 at 25fps, 1/100 at 50fps
  • Shallow depth of field for interviews (f/2.8 or wider where possible)

Interview Framing and Setup

  • Framing: Wider than a standard talking-head shot. Include from above the head (do not cut off the top of the head) down to the navel/midsection. Give the subject room in the frame.
  • Subject posture: Interviewee seated comfortably and leaning slightly toward the camera. Avoid stiff, upright posture.
  • Background: Position the subject well away from the back wall so the depth of field falls off naturally. Choose the far end of a room and keep the subject at a distance from it. Background should be visually interesting but soft and out of focus.

Stabilisation and Movement

  • Interviews: Tripod-mounted, stable. No movement during interviews.
  • B-roll: Gimbal-stabilised. No tripod for b-roll unless specifically required by the shot.
  • Movement in every b-roll shot: Sweeping side-to-side movements, reveals, and foreground elements to add depth. No static locked-off b-roll.
  • B-roll footage will often be slowed down further in the edit, so movements should be smooth and deliberate.
  • Handheld acceptable for b-roll only if movement is smooth and deliberate.

Audio

  • Record audio in-camera wherever possible. This saves significant time in post-production by avoiding the need to sync separate audio tracks.
  • Wireless lapel mic on each interviewee
  • Headphones for live monitoring on all interview takes
  • 30 seconds room tone recorded at Speak Out office
  • If a separate audio recorder is used, ensure a sync point (clap or slate) is captured on both devices

Lighting

  • Professional portrait lighting for all interviews. Not flat, not harsh. Shape the face.
  • Key light: LED panel or window light, slightly off-centre, with soft diffusion
  • Fill: bounce board or low-power LED to control contrast ratio
  • Background: practical lights or ambient separation from subject
  • Vary lighting direction between each of the three interview setups
  • B-roll: work with available light, supplement as needed
