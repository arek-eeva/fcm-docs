The brief

A film made for the room it will play in

This film has one audience that matters first: the CRH board, in Melbourne this October. It is an internal value proposition, not an advertisement, and it has to feel like a high-end production, because the board invests heavily in these moments and will read Adbri partly through how it presents itself.

So it cannot simply describe a process: mine the limestone, ship it, make cement, ship it again. A board does not need the steps. They need to feel the scale of what Adbri owns and understand the value behind each link in the chain: why the quarry matters, why the new vessel was worth the investment, why making cement here, the lowest-carbon way in the country, is an advantage a competitor cannot easily copy.

It is also a distinctly Australian story. Unlike CRH in Europe, Adbri moves materials by sea across great distances: limestone from Klein Point to Birkenhead, then cement on to Port Melbourne, before the network carries it across Victoria by road. That reach, and that integration, are the point.

The approach

Value at every link

Same locations and the same order as your storyboard, but the film argues a case rather than narrating a route. Each site answers a quiet question: why does this matter, and what value does it bring? That is what holds a board's attention, and it is what lets the sustainability story, lower-carbon cement and the electric-capable vessel, land as the answer to why us. Four things make it feel board-grade.

Scale you can feel

The board will never stand at Klein Point or walk Birkenhead. Aerial work gives them the physical size they will never see in person, with reveals that pull back until the machinery is a speck against the operation.

Light and colour

From cold first light over the quarry to golden hour as the cement reaches Melbourne, and the colour travels too: the bare reds, greys and yellows of the South Australian country giving way to the greener tones of Victoria.

Texture and atmosphere

Real manufacturing is dust, steel and grit. We lean into it. Backlit dust in the morning sun, the dark interiors of an old plant cut by shafts of light. Authentic and high-end at once.

The human side

Genuine, unposed portraits of the people who make this happen: the pride they take in the work, the mateship between them, the real skill each stage demands. Every frame compliant.

Sound carries as much as the picture. Under a restrained musical bed, real sound seeps through and grounds every location: seagulls over the jetty as the lime loads, waves against the hull of the silent ship, the deep roar of fire in the kiln. The voice is one of Australia's respected male artists, a mature and profound delivery the board settles into and trusts.

Beyond the boardroom

One shoot, designed to become many films

A board film that plays once and is never seen again is a wasted investment. We plan and shoot this so the footage keeps working long after October.

The same material, recut with a new narrative and voiceover, becomes your Employer Value Proposition and recruitment film: the "reinvent the way the world is built" story for the people you want to attract. It can feed an Australian overview of CRH, investor and town-hall content, and social and web cut-downs. We capture with that reuse in mind from the first day on site, in our framing, our coverage and our compliance, so nothing has to be reshot.

To make that practical, the project includes a Footage Library: the full set of master footage, organised and tagged by site and theme, colour-managed, backed up, and licensed for reuse. You are left with a living asset, not a single file.

The plan

Ten locations, two states, one tight schedule

  • Directed personally. I direct the film and am on site for the shoots, alongside a professional videographer and drone operator, with a professional photographer brought in where needed. The board film is led by the person who shaped the story, not handed down a chain.
  • Coverage, efficiently. Ten locations from quarry to customer across South Australia and Victoria. We pair sites into efficient days and use trusted local crews, so we are not flying gear and people around the country.
  • Compliance handled on site. A safety liaison on every shoot, to keep the crew safe and to make sure every frame is compliant: chin straps, sleeves, masks in the dusty areas, hair tied. Nothing the board sees will have a compliance problem.
  • The vessel, made easy. We film the Yampu loading and unloading; the in-transit footage you already hold is woven in, so there is no waiting on the ship's schedule.
  • Copyright, covered. Licensed music delivered with the certificate your legal team requires, as standard.
  • Approval, de-risked. A clear storyboard signed off up front, so the film that comes back is the film expected, then built to clear CRH review comfortably before October.
