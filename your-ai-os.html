The problem

Most AI forgets you the moment you close the tab.

You've probably tried ChatGPT or Claude. They're clever. But every new chat starts cold. You re-explain your business, your clients, your prices, your tone, over and over. It's a brilliant stranger who turns up with amnesia every single day.

The shift

Your Chief of Staff remembers. It knows how you work.

A Chief of Staff is the opposite of a blank chat. It holds everything about how you run your business: your priorities, your clients, your pricing, your voice. You stop briefing it, and start working with it.

What it is

It's a folder on your computer. That's it.

No new app to log into. No dashboard to learn. It's a folder that holds your business's knowledge, and an assistant that reads it, works in it, and keeps it up to date. It's yours, it's private, and it lives on your own machine.

Who it's for

For people who are great at the work, and buried in the admin.

Solo operators and small teams. Service businesses especially: consultants, agencies, advisers, trades, studios. If you're the one who turns the lights on and also writes every email, this is for you.

Why it works

It knows your priorities, your values, and how you think.

That's the difference between a tidy notepad and a real partner. Because it understands what you're chasing and what you believe, it puts the right things in front of you at the right time, and everything it writes sounds like you, not like a brochure.

Setting it up

You don't build it. A wizard builds it with you.

Open the folder, say "let's begin", and a patient guide walks you through it. It reads your website, learns from your old AI chats and your past invoices and proposals, and asks a few good questions. Small steps. Stop whenever you like. No technical skill needed.

Getting the most from it

Five habits that make it sing.

1
Start everything here. Make it the first place you go, before the blank page.
2
Feed it your history. The more it knows, the better it gets.
3
Correct it once. It remembers, so it won't make the same slip twice.
4
Keep it backed up. Put the folder in Dropbox, Drive, or iCloud.
5
Let it grow. It'll suggest the next useful step when you're ready.
