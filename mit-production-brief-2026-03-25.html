Scripts

Final scripts provided by Sara Tiefenbrun, 17 March 2026. Teleprompter delivery. Scripts can be adjusted during rehearsal so students feel relaxed and authentic.
Video 1 — Norrin (Welcome/Support)
Key message: MIT is supportive, you will feel at home.
Teleprompter delivery. Alternate tighter version.
Norrin On my first day at MIT, one thing really stood out — people are genuinely welcoming.

If I had a question, I could ask anyone and they'd help me on the spot, or point me in the right direction.

That support made settling in so much easier. I feel like I can depend on the staff and students.

Now when I walk into MIT, it just feels like home.
Video 2 — Norrin (Specialisation/IT)
Key message: MIT offers specialised IT and Business degrees, with like-minded students.
Teleprompter delivery.
Norrin Other institutions felt more general. MIT felt more focused on the subjects I'm interested in.

That was a big reason I chose it. It means the other students are interested in the same things I'm interested in.

I also liked that the learning is hands-on, not just theory.

With lectures, labs and tutorials, it feels practical and connected to where I want to go.
Video 3 — Nihriti (Personal/Social)
Key message: A smaller institution feels more personal and supportive.
Teleprompter delivery. Cut-down version.
Nihriti At MIT, it feels easy to connect.

The lecturers are approachable, the staff are friendly, and it's easy to get to know other students.

And because it's a smaller institution, it feels more personal.

For me, that makes studying feel more comfortable and more supportive.
Video 4 — Nihriti (Getting Involved)
Key message: It is easier to get involved at MIT because it is smaller and more personal.
Teleprompter delivery.
Nihriti At MIT, it's easy to step in and be part of things.

We often hear about opportunities through the team, like events or ways to take part.

And because the environment is smaller and more personal, it feels easier to take part.

For me that's a really important part of the experience.

You don't feel lost in the crowd — you feel like there's a place for you to step in and be involved.

Technical Settings

Camera

  • 4K, neutral profile, no baked LUT
  • Manual white balance — set per location
  • 25fps — all presenter / teleprompter shots
  • 50fps — all b-roll and action footage
  • Shutter: 1/50 at 25fps, 1/100 at 50fps
  • Frame horizontal (16:9). Verticals (9:16) and feed (4:5) created as crops in post. Frame subjects with crop margins in mind.

Support

  • Teleprompter — all scripted delivery
  • Gimbal — walk-and-talk, arrival sequences, campus movement
  • Tripod — static teleprompter setups, b-roll

Audio

  • 2x lav microphones (one per student, used alternately)
  • Headphones for live monitoring on all presenter takes
  • 30 seconds room tone recorded at each location
  • Shotgun mic for ambient/b-roll audio

Lighting

  • Mixed indoor environments (fluorescent + natural light)
  • Portable LED panel for fill on interview/teleprompter setups
  • Labs will have screen glow — plan controlled lighting
  • Reflector for fill in social/common areas
  • Bring gels for colour temperature matching if needed
