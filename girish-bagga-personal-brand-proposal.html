The bigger picture

Personal branding is a long game. Photographs are the starting point.

Done well, the photographs are just the visible layer. Underneath sits a body of content, a posting practice, a recognisable point of view, and an audience that grows because they trust who they are following.

At full bloom, your personal-brand setup could look like this 12 to 18 months from now:

  • A library of photographs that lets you post weekly on LinkedIn and Instagram without recycling the same image
  • Short-form video for reels and LinkedIn, where the camera follows you through your week and turns 5 to 10 minutes of footage into 30 to 60 days of social content
  • A speaker reel for keynote opportunities, built from event recordings as you grow into the Toastmasters work
  • A founder story film for the website and any speaking introduction, the kind of 90-second piece that makes someone want to know more before they have met you
  • Recurring photo top-ups every 6 to 12 months as the business evolves and the third clinic opens

None of that has to happen today. The right starting point is usually the photographs, because they make the posting possible, the posting builds the audience, and the audience earns the opportunity for everything that follows.

One half-day, two locations, around a dozen photographs.

Cheltenham clinic. The professional grounding. Shot with your business owner hat on, in the environment that gives those shots authority.

Mount Eliza home. The personal grounding. Lifestyle, reflective, the version of you that exists outside the clinic.

We are aiming for around 12 finished photographs covering three categories. The categories blend as we go. A great Instagram photo might cross all three. We shoot broadly and let the selection do the sorting.

Professional

4 to 5 photographs

The business owner version of you. Some on the clinic floor, some at reception, some seated, some standing. Considered, in command of the room. This is what goes on your LinkedIn headline image, the about section of your website, and the photograph attached to any speaking opportunity that comes your way.

Logistics

The practical bits.

When

Within the next 2 to 3 months. A weekday window works best for clinic access. We watch the weather and hold a backup date in case Mount Eliza is rained out.

Wardrobe

Three to four tops covering professional, smart casual, and active. Same pants are fine; the camera lives above the waist most of the time. Lay them out before the day, we mix and match.

Props

Whatever feels honest. Laptop, tablet, phone, the book you are currently reading (Shoe Dog works, by the way). If a watch, bag, or piece of equipment is part of how you show up, bring it.

Clinic access

Cheltenham only. Narre Warren is harder with the shared building. Ideally a quiet block of an hour or so when the floor is not full. Coordinate with your team ahead of time.
