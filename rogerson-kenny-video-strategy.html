01
Why this, why now

Two moments are landing on top of each other

Fifty years since Peter opened the doors. A move out of the building you have been in for a generation. Either one is a brand moment in itself. Together, they are a one-time-only window, and we have eight to ten weeks to act on it before that window closes.

You and Mark have been talking about video for years. The opportunity now is not to finally produce a few. It is to use this moment to reset the way Rogerson Kenny shows up on screen, so what people see online matches the firm I heard you describe. Fifty years deep. Long relationships. Three pillars working under one roof.

In financial services, trust is the only product that matters, and trust is built on what people see. Right now the site shows up with stock images and basic snapshots. That contradicts everything you stand for. The move is the natural reason to fix it.

01
Fifty Years

This story only lives because Peter is still around. Long-term clients are still around. The original 1976 office is still standing five metres from your meeting room. We capture this once, properly, and we have it forever. Miss the window and most of it goes with the move.

02
New Home

Mulgrave is more than a logistical change. It is a reset, an invitation, and a story all in one, but only if we treat it that way from day one.

03
Trust at Scale

What I have learned working in financial services is that authentic, well-produced video is the one thing that lets a firm of 35 people feel personal at every touchpoint. Stock imagery does the opposite. My job is to remove it everywhere it shows up.

04
Reactive Capacity

Most firms commission a bunch of video and then go quiet for two years. We will not. The plan ends with a small studio installed at the new office so Mark, Sarah, or anyone else can produce content the moment they have something worth saying. No re-engaging me. No scheduling a crew.

03
Production Approach

How I would approach it

You have already seen a fair bit of what we did for Aaron. Where I can in this document, I am pointing you to other formats Aaron's project did not include, so you can see how the same thinking scales beyond the customer experience video.

Strategy first. Production follows purpose. Then everything we shoot feeds three content pillars: brand message, testimonials, and educational content. Brand message earns the first impression. Testimonials prove it. Educational content keeps you visible between visits. Each one shows up later in the plan.

Strategy First

Production follows purpose

Every video I make starts with one question. What is it for, who is it for, what should it make them do? The plan gets locked before we touch a camera. By the time we shoot, every minute on set is paying back in clarity afterwards. Less time on you. More useful footage. No waste.

Half-Day Foundations

Capture once, edit forever

One half-day at the old office, one half-day at the new, plus the launch night. That is enough footage to feed a year of brand content across all three pillars. The same shoot that makes the videos also fills your photo library.

No Stock Imagery
