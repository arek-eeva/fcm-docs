Overview

One production day focused on volume and variety. The goal: give Spicy Web (CGT's Meta agency) a library of short-form content with enough variety to A/B test hooks, products, and formats across campaigns. Spicy Web tests what works, then we produce more of what wins.

Everything shot today should be authentic, natural, and true to the craft community. The handmade audience is highly attuned to anything that feels forced or produced for production's sake. Natural lighting, genuine reactions, real workspace. That is the brief.

Content types: B-roll library, short-form ad creatives (multiple hook variants), FAQ and how-to videos, talking-head product showcases, and some playful process content. Multiple setups, multiple products. Aim to be versatile across the day rather than perfecting any single piece.

Phase 1 is volume-focused ad content. Phase 2 (later in 2026, after the move to new premises) covers brand story, Mirboo North location footage, and the full founder narrative. Keep today's focus tight.

Content Types to Capture

A
B-Roll Library
Slow motion, no audio
The foundation of everything. Shoot this across all three locations and throughout the day, not just at the start. Good B-roll is what editors reach for. Prioritise variety: different angles, different distances, different subjects.
  • Workshop environment: shelving, products arranged by colour, textures, materials
  • Team at work: packing, organising, discussing. Dyeing process: snippets only, not full process (trade secrets apply)
  • Product close-ups: thread colours, Takadisi shapes, Field Journal fabrics, Liberty prints
  • Hands working: stitching, cutting, arranging, handling thread
  • Team of women in aprons and gloves, working naturally
  • Workshop details: equipment, labels, supplies, the organised chaos of a real working studio
  • New space walkthrough or exterior if access is confirmed
  • Scenic footage driving into Mirboo North, captured en route
B
Short-Form Ad Creatives
30–60 seconds each
Multiple versions of each piece, with different hooks. The core content stays the same. The opening 10 seconds changes. Spicy Web will test which hooks drive the best results and brief more content based on what wins.

Myth-bust"This is why your [current approach] isn't working"

IYKYK"This is very 'if you know, you know'"

C
FAQ Videos
1–2 minutes each
Katie answering common customer questions. Natural, conversational tone. These are quick to shoot: one question, one answer, one take (with a few attempts if needed). Katie to bring a written or mental list of her most frequently asked questions. Film as many as time allows.
D
How-To Tips and Tricks
1–2 minutes each
Short, practical demonstrations. These perform well in the craft community because they deliver real value quickly. Film at least two or three of these.
  • Product storage and organisation tips
  • How to start with Takadisi shapes
  • Thread care and preparation
  • The packaging and unpacking experience (satisfying for social)
E
Talking-Head Product Showcases
2–4 minutes each
Katie presenting priority products. Why she created them, what makes them special, who they're for. These are the pieces Spicy Web can cut into multiple shorter ads. Film the priority products (1–3 above) in this format first, before moving to anything else.
F
Secret Process Content
30–60 seconds
Playful snippets of the dyeing process with "censored" or pixelated overlays added in post on any trade-secret steps. Creates intrigue, signals expertise, and gives audiences a reason to keep watching. Works as a hook and as a standalone organic post.

Important: Do NOT film the full dyeing process. Always check with Katie before filming any process step. Capture snippets: hands, colours in water, results. Not method.

Wardrobe and Styling

  • No uniforms. The audience expects to see a real working studio, not a branded shoot. Workwear is the right call.
  • Aprons and gloves when dyeing — that is the natural costume and it reads as authentic.
  • Katie to bring 2–3 different tops to swap between setups. Same person, different look. Content appears shot on multiple occasions.
  • No lab coats. They read as "chemicals" and conflict with the natural, handmade brand positioning.
  • The rest of the team wears whatever they normally wear. Best-presenting version of their usual workwear is the goal.

Key Production Notes

Trade Secrets

The dyeing process involves proprietary techniques developed over 18 years. Film snippets and stages, never the full process. Playful "censored" or pixelated overlays can be added in post for intrigue. Always check with Katie before filming any process step.

Camera-Shy Talent

Katie has flagged she gets awkward on camera. This is normal. Every client says this. Be patient, keep it conversational, build confidence gradually. Her existing content proves she is excellent on camera. She just needs time to warm up before the first take matters.

Small Spaces

Both the workshop and home studio are compact. Bring small equipment and own lighting. Plan the camera placement before setting up anything else. Be ready to rethink the setup if the space doesn't allow it.

Music and Tone

The previous agency used jarring music choices. CGT's audience is the craft community — warm, authentic, community-driven. Music choices must reflect that. Nothing corporate. Nothing stock. Confirm music approach with Katie before edits are delivered.

Authenticity is Non-Negotiable

The handmade and craft community instantly rejects anything that feels fake, polished for its own sake, or AI-generated. Confirmed at the US trade show in Nashville (September 2025): AI in front-facing content is "suicide" in this space. Natural lighting, real reactions, real workspace. That is what this audience trusts. That is what we produce.
