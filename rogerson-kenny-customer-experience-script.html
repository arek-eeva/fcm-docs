Customer experience video · 90 seconds · Tue 21 July shoot

You picked The Tour Guide because you wanted to share the load, rather than tie the firm to one person's identity. We've taken that all the way through this refinement.

The script still carries the warm institutional voice. What's new: a quick team-in-motion opener that establishes Rogerson Kenny as a team before a single word is spoken, a Monash Freeway anchor in the welcome so clients know exactly where Mulgrave sits, tighter wayfinding that respects how visitor parking actually works, and a production approach where the reader appears on camera at the open and close only, with B-roll covering the middle.

Have a read, and if it sits well, all we need from you is who'd like to be the reader on the day. We've left a couple of small open questions at the bottom for you to land before we lock it a week out from the shoot.

What changed since you last saw it

  • Team-in-motion opener (visual). Two to three seconds of staff at work, conversations, a coffee being put down. Shares the load before the script even starts.
  • Monash Freeway anchor. Added to the welcome so Greater-Melbourne clients know exactly where the new office sits.
  • Reframed the parking line. Now reads "A visitor spot will be reserved for you. We'll confirm which one when you book in." Matches how booking actually works.
  • Cut the dead-end road mention. The visuals show the reveal naturally; saying it aloud added unnecessary friction.
  • Cut the shared-carpark explanation. Straight directions to the 633 lifts instead, with branded pillars confirming clients are in the right place.
  • Production approach locked in. Reader appears on camera at open and close only. Middle is B-roll over her voice. Teleprompter direct-to-lens read on the day.

Production Notes
Teleprompter

Direct-to-lens, single take

The reader records the entire script on camera with a teleprompter sitting just above the lens. The on-camera audio doubles as the voice-over for the wayfinding middle, so we capture clean audio for the whole video in one take.

On-camera reveal

Open and close only

The reader appears on camera for the welcome and the closing line. Everything between is B-roll covering the journey, with her voice carrying it. Keeps the visual storytelling focused on the space and the team, while her presence bookends the experience with warmth.

Open questions for you

Two small things before we lock.

  1. Who reads? You mentioned Melissa or Sarah, whichever feels most natural and would enjoy the on-camera moment. Either has the warmth and authority to carry the script. Sarah is already on camera that day for the brand message interview, so she's a clean double-up; Melissa brings the marketing-led voice. Your call.
  2. Any final line tweaks? If anything reads off when you say it aloud, send it back and we'll work it through with you. Otherwise we'll lock the script a week before the shoot, with everything finalised before the day.
