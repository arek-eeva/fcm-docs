The Shape of the System.

How to think about your Chief of Staff before you build one. The architecture, the habits, and the shortest road to the moment it clicks.

Part two · AI That Actually Works was the why · this is the how

Presented by Arek Rainczuk
Belgrave · Victoria
02 · The Frame

A Hire, Not an Install.

The one frame that decides whether this works: you're not setting up software, you're onboarding a colleague.

01 · The Trap
Treat it like an app
Install, poke at the settings, expect it to know your business. It can't. A chat window has never met your clients, your prices, or your voice, and it forgets you the moment you close it.
02 · The Frame
Treat it like a hire
A sharp colleague with total amnesia: capable from day one, blank every morning. What would you do for that hire? You'd write everything down and leave it on their desk.
03 · The System
The folder cures the amnesia
Plain files it reads at the start of every session. Everything you'd tell a great hire in their first week, written down once, read every single day.

If you can write it down, the system can act on it.

03 · The Architecture

The Anatomy.

A handful of folders is the whole architecture. Plain files, plain English, nothing hidden. You can open and read every one of them.

The brain

One operating brief. Who you are, what you sell, your prices, your voice, your priorities. Read at the start of every session.

The memory

Facts it keeps, plus a daily log of what happened. Tomorrow starts where today finished, not from zero.

The hands

Trackers for tasks and pipeline, playbooks for the work you repeat, templates for the documents you send.

04 · The Architecture

Two Speeds of Truth.

Every fact in the system moves at one of two speeds. Keep them apart and it stays calm.

Stable · changes rarely

What the business is · touched monthly, maybe

  • What you sell and what it costs
  • Your values and your strategic filter
  • Brand: colours, fonts, how you sound
  • How you like to work
lives in → CLAUDE.md, brand/
Live · changes daily

What's happening now · touched every session

  • Which jobs are on and where they're up to
  • This week's focus and blockers
  • Who owes you money
  • What got decided today
lives in → trackers, Logs/, a current-state note

Mix them and the brief goes stale while you re-explain your own business every morning. Separate them and the brief stays true while the day moves.

05 · The Foundation

Where It Lives.

One decision, made once, before anything is installed. Storage and live work are different jobs, and the failure happens when one tool is asked to do both.

01 · Live Work
Local, always
The system folder sits on your computer's own disk, in your home folder. Not inside iCloud, Google Drive or Dropbox. And watch the Desktop: many Macs quietly sync Desktop and Documents to iCloud, so "local" isn't where you think it is.
02 · Backup
Deliberate, separate
Time Machine to a drive, or a zipped snapshot dropped into your cloud drive. A synced folder is not a backup: a deletion syncs just as faithfully as a save.
03 · Assets
Cloud, as always
Big files, deliverables, client files stay in Drive or Dropbox. Storing finished work is exactly the job those tools are built for. Leave them to it.

Live work local. Backup deliberate. Assets in the cloud. Three jobs, three places.

06 · The Habit

The Rhythm Is the Engine.

The architecture only pays off through one daily loop. Day one it knows what you wrote down. Day thirty it knows your clients, your pipeline, and how you like things done.

The session ends with the system knowing more than it did this morning. That's the whole trick.

and tomorrow starts where today finished

For the first month, start everything in the system, even what feels quicker elsewhere. You're not saving time yet. You're training your replacement for the boring parts.

07 · The Habit

Write It Down Once.

A playbook is a procedure in your own words: how you quote, what goes in your project brief, the questions you ask a new lead, your prices and your rules.

01 · The Rule
The rule of three
Done something three times? Write it down. That's a playbook now, and you never start it from scratch again.
02 · The Payoff
Procedures, not prompts
"Do a proposal for Sarah" now runs your whole procedure: your structure, your prices, your tone, your checklist. One sentence in, finished document out.
03 · The Start
Three come in the kit
Job prep, proposal, project brief. Rewrite them until they sound like you, then add your own as the rule of three catches them.

This is where the hours come back. Every playbook is a job you'll never explain twice.

08 · The Breakthrough

The First Hour.

Don't set up everything. The goal of the first sitting is one real piece of work, as fast as possible. Everything else can wait.

01 · Begin
Say "let's begin"
The wizard interviews you, gently. Your website does half the talking, ten minutes of questions does the rest. You talk, it builds.
02 · Import
The handover trick
Your existing AI chats already know a surprising amount about your business. The wizard shows you how to export that knowledge and fold it in: months of context in one move.
03 · Win
One real task, today
Before the first sitting ends, it does something real for your business: a quote, a brief, an email in your voice. Feel it work before you invest another minute.

The breakthrough isn't a feature. It's the first output you'd actually send.

10 · Together

Same Shape, Shared Parts.

This is why we're all installing one architecture instead of ten different ones.

Swap playbooks

A pricing playbook written in one business drops into another with the names changed. Same shape, so the parts fit.

Compare briefs

When something works, we can see why, because we're all looking at the same files in the same places.

Build together

Good brains in this room. An idea sketched on that whiteboard today can be in everyone's system by Friday.
