The Opportunity

Volume and variety

CGT is scaling its Meta ad campaigns with Spicy Web. To do that well, Spicy Web needs a library of authentic, high-quality video content featuring Katie on camera, with enough variety to test across audiences, placements, and messaging angles.

This proposal covers Phase 1: a focused production day designed to deliver maximum usable content in a single session. Katie's product knowledge, humour, and genuine connection to the craft community are CGT's greatest creative assets. This shoot is built around that.

We are asking CGT and Spicy Web to drive the content. You know your products and your customers. Tell us which products to feature, which questions to answer, and what stories to tell. We will handle the rest: direction, production, and editing that gives Spicy Web everything they need to run and test.

Phase 1

Ad content production

A volume-driven shoot day across two locations, capturing as many usable content pieces as possible for Meta campaigns and organic channels.

Product Demo / How-To

Katie demonstrating priority products on camera. Close-up detail, practical and educational. 1-2 minutes each. These show customers exactly how a product works and why it is worth buying.

Products filmed in use and being made, where possible.

Short-Form Ad Creative

Hook-driven videos built for Meta placements. 15-60 seconds, structured to stop the scroll in the first three seconds. Multiple hooks per topic where footage allows. Purpose-built for paid social, not repurposed longer content.

Q&A / FAQ Videos

Katie answering real customer questions about products, shipping, education, and USPs. 1-2 minutes each. Minimal editing by design. These build trust and reduce purchase friction, especially for US customers.

Longer-Form Content

2+ minute videos that can be cut into multiple shorter ad variants for campaign testing. Founder-led storytelling, product deep-dives, or project-based content showing the full journey from thread to finished piece.

What We Capture on Shoot Day

Products being used (stitching, demonstrating, finished pieces). Products being made (dyeing process, quality, hands-on craft). Multiple hooks and openers per topic for A/B testing. Close-up detail shots, progress and transformation clips, and supporting b-roll for ad overlays. All footage is professionally edited and delivered. We also retain the raw footage library for future use, so new cuts can be made without the cost of another shoot.

Production Details

How it works

Crew: Arek Rainczuk (Director) and Sam Young (Videographer).

Locations: CGT workspace, Mirboo North, and Katie's home studio.

Shoot format: Horizontal (16:9) as the primary filming format, with the option to crop into vertical (9:16) and square (1:1) for different placements.

Style: Lightly edited, natural, authentic. No over-polished ad look. Katie's genuine voice and delivery are the creative asset.

Delivery: Edited videos delivered within one to three weeks, depending on our current workload, speed of revisions, and any additional material we need from CGT. One round of revisions included per video. Raw footage library retained for future use.

Before We Shoot

What we need from you

To make the most of the shoot day, we need a few things confirmed ahead of time. The more prepared we are, the more content we can capture.

For CGT Katie & Angela

  1. Which products are the priority for this shoot? (Specific thread collections, Tack It Easy shapes, kits)
  2. Which products will Spicy Web be running ads for first?
  3. What are the most common customer questions you get? (For Q&A videos: product use, colour selection, US shipping, paper piecing tips, thread quality)
  4. Are there any products being made on shoot day we can film? (Dyeing, packaging, quality checks)
  5. Do you have finished project examples we can film as "after" shots? (Completed embroidery, patchwork pieces)
  6. Any upcoming launches, seasonal themes, or collections we should tie content to?
  7. Who needs to be on camera? Just Katie, or anyone else?
  8. Any topics or angles you want to make sure we cover?
  9. Can you have the priority products prepared and ready for filming on the day? (Thread collections laid out, Tack It Easy shapes set up, any finished project examples on hand)

For Spicy Web Ashleigh

  1. What are the priority ad formats and placements? (Reels, Stories, Feed, Advantage Plus)
  2. Confirmed specs: vertical (9:16) primary, croppable to 1:1 and 16:9?
  3. Are there specific hooks or messaging angles you want us to test?
  4. What is your ideal number of finished ad creatives from this shoot?
  5. Do you need raw footage delivered as well, or only edited pieces?
  6. What is your turnaround expectation for edited content?
  7. Any performance benchmarks or creative references we should see before the shoot?
  8. What aspect ratios do you need for each placement? We will film in 16:9 and can crop to 9:16 and 1:1. Are there any other formats required?
