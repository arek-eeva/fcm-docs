The Film

Not a testimonial. A story.

This film follows Joe's story: a man who worked hard his whole life and finally set out to build something lasting, without cutting corners. An ambitious project like this needs partners you can genuinely trust, and that is where Steve and Ali come in.

We are positioning three people along that arc. Joe is the heart of it, the developer realising a lifelong ambition. Steve is the trusted name Joe reached out to, the person who looked after him and opened the right door. Ali is the specialist who did the heavy lifting and helped make it happen. Bowery sits inside the story as the partner Joe could trust, rather than the subject of a pitch.

The questions and the look both serve that. We want honest, unhurried conversation and a premium, architectural feel. Closer to property editorial than corporate.

Interview Guide

Five questions for each

Conversation starters, not a script. Nobody needs to memorise anything or prepare polished lines. The best material comes from people just talking honestly, and we will guide it on the day. Sharing these ahead simply helps everyone relax.

JoeThe developer
  1. You've worked hard your whole life. What did you want this home to stand for, after everything you've built?
  2. You could have taken an easier or cheaper path. Why did doing it properly, with no shortcuts, matter so much to you?
  3. When you talk about leaving a legacy, what do you actually picture, and who is it for?
  4. A project this ambitious meant trusting other people with your vision. What were you looking for in the partners you let in?
  5. Standing in the finished home now, what does it mean to you?
  6. Before you reached out to Steve, what had you heard about him, and what made you trust him enough to pick up the phone?
  7. When it came to the hardest part of all this, how much did Ali help you, and what did he take off your shoulders?
SteveThe trusted introduction
  1. When Joe first came to you, what did you see in him and in what he was trying to build?
  2. Joe reached out because he trusted you. What does carrying that kind of trust mean to you?
  3. What made you believe in this project enough to stand behind it?
  4. You introduced Joe to Ali. What made you sure Ali was the right person for this one?
  5. Seeing what Joe has created, how does it feel to have opened that door?
  6. Once Joe came to you, how did you look after him through it?
  7. Why did you bring Ali in the way you did, and what made him the right fit for what Joe needed?
AliThe specialist who made it happen
  1. What did you take on for Joe, and what made this project a genuine test?
  2. Where did the hardest work happen, and what did it take to get it through?
  3. When things got difficult, how did you and Joe get through it together?
  4. What does it mean to you to help someone turn a vision this personal into something real?
  5. When you look at the finished home, what are you most proud of?
  6. When Steve first brought this to you, what did you understand about Joe and what he wanted?
  7. You could see Joe would take no shortcuts. What made you realise you had to step up for him?
What We'll Capture

The shoot

Interviews

Two cameras, lapel mics, soft lighting. Filmed against the home itself, no studio backdrop.

Walk-through

Joe hosting, showing Steve and Ali through the home and pointing out the details and finishes.

Architectural b-roll

Slow, considered detail of staircases, kitchens, bathrooms, finishes and the penthouse spaces.

Exteriors & drone

Heritage facade, the streetscape and the home's place in the city. Drone weather and airspace permitting.

Deliverables: a story film of roughly two to three minutes, with an optional 60 to 90 second social cut-down.
