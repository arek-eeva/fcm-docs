What it is

The problem isn't your workload. It's that only you hold the map.

Every time you open a new chat, you start from scratch: re-explaining your pricing, your clients, your tone, your way of doing things. You get a decent answer, copy it somewhere, close the tab, and tomorrow nothing has moved. The disarray stays yours to manage.

A Chief of Staff works the other way. It holds a working memory for how you run things, and it does the actual work: drafting, updating, tracking, writing in your voice. Change your price today and it carries that through your documents, your website and your emails from that moment. A decision made is a decision implemented. It lives on your own machine, private and yours, and we build it with you until you can run and extend it yourself.

Straight answers

The questions founders ask.

Won't this be out of date in six months? AI moves so fast.
The opposite. This isn't an app that ships once and dates. It's a set of instructions that runs on whatever the best model is that week. When a sharper model or a better harness lands, your Chief of Staff gets sharper the same day, no rebuild and no new invoice. Most tools lose value the moment you buy them. This one compounds.
What if I want out, or you disappear? Am I locked in?
No lock-in, by design. It lives on your own machine, Mac or PC, written in plain instructions you own, not buried on someone else's servers. It runs on Claude today and can be moved to another AI system down the track, with your Chief of Staff walking you through it. There's no retainer holding you in. My whole job is to make myself unnecessary, and I can't do that if I've trapped you.
I'm not technical. Can I actually run this?
Your three jobs are talk, record, approve. You describe how your business works, I build it around you and hand it over, and if it ever needs to move or change, your Chief of Staff sorts it out with you. If you can explain your business out loud, you can run this.
I already use ChatGPT. How is this different?
Chat is level one. You re-explain your context every time and it answers a question. A Chief of Staff holds your whole business in memory and moves the work: a decision made becomes a decision carried through every system that depends on it. A chatbot answers. This does the job.
It's built bespoke to me. Doesn't that mean I'm stuck with how you set it up?
The opposite of stuck. Because it's a set of instructions made bespoke to you, you can point it at any part of your business and run it however you need: marketing this week, finance next, a new hire's onboarding the week after. It bends to you, and it keeps bending as you grow.
