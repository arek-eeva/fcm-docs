What you'd be running.

Good to catch up. You asked three fair questions: what actually gets installed, how it touches NetSuite and Outlook, and rough pricing. Here it is in plain terms, enough to take to your IT team, without the parts that make it ours.

What it is

Your Chief of Staff is Claude Code, Anthropic's desktop app, working inside a single folder on your own machine. It's not a new platform to log into and it's not a service that holds your data. It's a way of working: the AI reads, writes and organises real files in that one folder, and acts on your behalf under your direction.

What actually gets installed

  • The Claude desktop app (Anthropic)

    One application, from Anthropic. It includes Claude Code, the part that can work with files. That's the whole install. Vendor is Anthropic, the makers of Claude.

  • An optional Claude Chrome extension

    Only if you want it. It lets the assistant drive a web app in your browser, clicking and typing as you would, for anything without a proper backend connection. Optional, and easy to leave off.

  • Nothing inside NetSuite or Outlook

    It doesn't install anything into your other systems. It connects to them, covered below, rather than living in them.

Where your data lives

On your computer, in that one folder, as plain text files you can open and read yourself. It only ever sees what you point it at. Nothing is uploaded to us, and nothing is published anywhere unless you explicitly choose to.

How it connects

  • NetSuite

    Two paths. If your IT enables the NetSuite connector for Claude, it works through a validated API, no screen-scraping, faster and cleaner. Until that's switched on, the Chrome extension drives NetSuite in your browser manually: slower and more awkward, but it gets the job done in your test environment. Your call, and IT's, on which.

  • Outlook / Microsoft 365

    Optional. It can read and draft replies for you to approve and send. Microsoft keeps third-party access fairly locked down (Copilot is the fully-open path inside their world), so this is the most constrained connection, and entirely up to you whether we wire it in at all.

  • The two Chrome tools you already run

    It sits alongside them, not on top. Where they stop, it can pick up, and it's far less limited by design than the deliberately cautious tools you're working with now.

For your IT and security team

The short version to hand across, given your SOC 2 obligations:

  • Local-first. Runs on your machine, scoped to a single folder you choose. It can't see anything outside it.
  • You approve actions. It works under your direction and can be set to ask before it does anything that touches a live system.
  • Validated connections preferred. Where a proper API or connector exists (like NetSuite's), we use that over browser automation, which is cleaner and easier to audit.
  • Secrets stay out. Logins and sensitive credentials are kept out of the system, not stored in the files.
  • One vendor to assess: Anthropic (Claude). What to review with IT: the Claude desktop app, the optional Chrome extension, and whether to enable the NetSuite connector.

Happy to jump on a call with your IT team directly if that's easier than a forwarded page.

The bit you care about

The demo data setup: pulling product images from a company's site and creating those products in the ERP for a demo. It can do exactly that, through the NetSuite connector once IT enables it, or through Chrome in the meantime. You set it running, go and grab a coffee, come back to it done.
