Your homework, Ken.

Good to talk this morning. Before our next session, about an hour of gathering, so we spend our time together building, not digging. Nothing here needs tidying. Rough and real is exactly right, and the more of your actual work you bring, the more Kenny Pops knows you from the very first minute.

1. One folder, called Kenny Pops

This is the only bit of setup. Make one folder and use it as a dumping ground. Everything goes in here, any format, in no order. Kenny Pops sorts it out later.

  1. Right-click on your desktop.
  2. Choose New Folder.
  3. Name it Kenny Pops.

Everything you add is a copy. The originals stay exactly where they are.

2. Drop in your work

A copy of each into the Kenny Pops folder. A few of each is plenty, don't go hunting for everything.

  • Your webinar decks

    Even just the last one or two. You don't need to dig out all forty, a couple shows Kenny Pops how you teach and how you build a session.

  • Your KTG newsletters

    A few recent Ken Talks GST issues. This is your voice on the page, which is exactly what we want it to learn.

  • Your LinkedIn posts

    Copy a handful you're happy with into a document, or just paste the links. Both work.

  • Your website words

    The copy from your new site. It carries your positioning and how you talk about what you do.

  • The paperwork you do have

    Your one-page engagement letter, your terms, and a couple of invoices. Small things, but they teach it how you package and bill your work.

  • Your prices and what's included

    Whatever form it's in. So anything client-facing comes out consistent.

Most of your work is verbal, and that's fine. We're not after notes. Kenny Pops will interview you next session to capture what only lives in your head. This step is just the things you already have lying around.

3. Bring your AI work across

You've already done real work inside Claude, Perplexity and ChatGPT, your Fehily Advisory project and the others. Let's carry it over rather than start cold. Paste the prompt below into each project, let it write a clean handover, and save what comes back into the Kenny Pops folder.

Paste this into each AI project 
You're helping me hand over to a new AI assistant that will run the business side of my advisory practice. Using everything you know about me and my work from our chats and this project, write a clear, plain handover brief a new assistant could read to get up to speed.

Cover these, with headings:
1. About me and my work: who I am, what I do, and one line on what makes my approach distinctive.
2. What I offer and what it costs: my services, and pricing if you know it.
3. Who I serve: my clients and audience, and the problem I help with.
4. My voice: how I write and speak, the words and tone I use, with two short examples in my style.
5. What we've worked on together: the projects, drafts and tasks you've helped me with, and where each is up to.
6. My recurring tasks: the things I do regularly that an assistant could take off my plate.
7. My preferences: how I like to work, and what I do and don't want from an assistant.
8. Anything else important you know about my work.

Write it as one document I can copy out in full.

Important: keep it to the business layer only. Do not include any client names, case details, or anything private about the people I work with. If something can't be shared, leave it out and say so.

Then:

  1. Run it once in each of your projects, across Claude, Perplexity and ChatGPT. Each one remembers different things, so each will give you a different brief.
  2. Run it once in a normal chat too, to catch anything held in general memory.
  3. Copy each result into a document and save it into the Kenny Pops folder. A separate file for each is perfectly fine, no need to name them neatly.

You set the line

Kenny Pops is built for the business layer of your practice: your marketing, your newsletters and decks, your content, admin, templates and correspondence. It does not hold client tax files or anything that identifies a client.

You know your obligations better than anyone, so you draw the line. As you gather samples, keep client-identifying detail out, swap in a placeholder or take that part out first.

And to put your mind at rest: this all lives on your own computer. Nothing is sent anywhere. You decide what goes in, what stays out stays out entirely, and we won't go near your email unless one day you decide you want that.
