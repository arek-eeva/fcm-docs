Organising your workspace.

You'll have a lot of sessions before long, some finished, some mid-build, some waiting on you. A few small habits keep the whole thing tidy and let it work for you while you're away. Everything here lives in the session list on the left.

The status dots (read the list at a glance)

Each session in the list carries a small coloured dot. It tells you where that conversation stands without opening it.

Blue. Done, or waiting on nothing. Nothing needed from you.

Orange. It has a question and needs your input before it can carry on.

Scan down the list and jump to the orange ones first. This is how you run several things at once, they work away, and the dots tell you who needs you.

Fork a session (explore two directions at once)

On a session in the list, click the three dots, then Fork. It makes a copy that carries all the same context up to that point.

Use it when one conversation is pulling two ways: fork it, take one copy down one path and the other down the second, without re-explaining the background to either. Like a fork in the road, same starting point, two journeys.

Pin the ones you keep coming back to

Right-click a session and choose Pin. It sticks to the top of the list so you're not scrolling to find the build you're halfway through, or the one you're waiting on.

Unpin it the same way once you're done with it.

Rename so you can find them

Three dots on a session, then Rename. The name it gives itself is fine, but a clear one, "Folk CRM cleanup," "Scaling plan," "Website refresh," saves you hunting later.

Group them into folders

Right-click a session, then Move to group, then New group. Name it, Marketing, CRM, Setup, whatever fits, and you've made a folder.

Drag other sessions straight into it, or use the three dots and Add to group. Fold a group closed to get it out of sight. Once you've got more than a handful of sessions, this is what stops the list becoming a mess.

A side question (without cluttering the main chat)

Mid-build, you sometimes want to ask a quick thing that isn't part of the job, "wait, what did that plan say about the pricing?" Type /btw (by the way) and ask it in a side chat.

It still sees everything from the main conversation, so it answers with full context, but the exchange stays off to the side and nothing folds back into the main thread. The main build stays clean.

Routines (jobs that run on a schedule)

Top-left, Routines. This is where you set up work that runs itself on a schedule, a morning brief, a weekly tidy, whatever earns its place. There are templates to start from, or describe your own.

Two things to get right, or it runs dumb or at the wrong hour:

Set the model and effort. A routine can quietly default to the fast, cheap setting. For anything that needs judgement, set it to Opus and a higher effort so it's actually sharp.

Set your timezone. Tell it your timezone (Melbourne) so a "7am" job runs at 7am your time, not somewhere else.

Routines work best if your computer stays on and the app stays open, so it's there to run when the time comes.

Don't set all of this up at once. Add a group or a pin the moment the list starts to feel busy, and set up a routine once you've got a job worth repeating.
