Backing it up.

Your Chief of Staff lives in a folder on your computer. Two simple safety nets keep that folder safe and reachable from anywhere. Here's the setup I'd use.

Two kinds of safety net

A live synced copy, so you never lose work, and you can reach your files from your phone or another computer.

A version history, so you can roll back a bad change, not just keep the latest one.

You want one of each. They cover different kinds of bad day.

The live copy: Dropbox or Google Drive

Both sync your folder in the background and give you the files on any device. Pick one.

Dropbox is what I use, and it's rock solid for this. Google Drive does the same job well if you already live in Google.

The version history: GitHub

GitHub quietly keeps a snapshot of every change. Made a mess? Roll back to how it was this morning in seconds.

This is the net the others can't give you. Dropbox and Drive only hold the latest version, so a bad edit syncs the mess everywhere. GitHub remembers every step.

Your Chief of Staff does the saving for you. Just keep the repo private, it's your whole business in there.

Skip iCloud, for this folder

iCloud can quietly swap your real files for download-on-demand placeholders to save space, which breaks the tools that need the actual file sitting there. Fine for photos, not for a working folder. Leave this one out.

Two rules that keep sync happy

One machine at a time. Don't edit the same files from two computers at once, or you'll get "conflicted copy" duplicates. Finish on one, then move to the other.

Don't drown it in tiny files. Thousands of little files all changing at once can choke the sync. Keep the count sensible, and let your Chief of Staff keep things tidy.

Keep secrets out of all of it. Tokens and passwords never belong in anything that syncs or backs up. (See Driving Claude Code for the safe way.)

The sweet spot: Dropbox or Drive so you never lose work, GitHub so you can undo a bad day. Your Chief of Staff will set both up with you.
