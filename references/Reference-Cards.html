Build your own Chief of Staff

Reference cards.

The handful of guides worth keeping. Open any one whenever you need it, no need to read them front to back.

  • Driving Claude Code →
    The controls that sit below the chat box: plan and auto mode, shaping a plan before it runs, choosing the model (and the sticky-default trap), the context meter, previewing changes, feedback, and private tokens.
  • Organising Your Workspace →
    Keep the session list tidy and let it work while you're away: the status dots, forking, pinning, renaming, groups, the "by the way" side chat, and scheduled routines.
  • Briefing Your Chief of Staff →
    How you brief it is the whole difference. Give a brief not a question, correct it once, be specific, and talk rather than type.
  • Building Something →
    Your build loop, start to finish: vision first, plan it together, build with Opus and a team, get a Codex audit, then tidy up and go again.
  • Getting Unstuck →
    When you hit a wall: paste the error, fix a stale file, the terminal, updating Claude Code, and when to call.
  • Backing It Up →
    Keep your system safe and reachable: a live synced copy (Dropbox or Drive) plus version history (GitHub), and what to steer clear of.
