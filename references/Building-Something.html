Building something.

An app, a system, or a single skill, the same path works. Start with the end in mind, not the how. Your Chief of Staff works out the how with you.

1Start with the vision

Don't worry about how it gets built. Picture the finished thing as clearly as you can: what it does, what it looks like, what it's like to use.

Whether it's an app, a system, or one skill, describe the end result you want. The "how" is your Chief of Staff's job, not yours.

2Plan it together (plan-first mode)

Switch to plan-first mode and lay out your vision. Ask your Chief of Staff to suggest how to build it, to research the options, and to work out any extra tools or software you'll need.

Then go back and forth. Question anything that's off, ask for alternatives, refine. Don't start building until the plan feels right.

And don't forget to ask your Chief of Staff to grill you, with questions, until the vision is properly clear. Half-formed in your head becomes clear on the page.

The time you spend here pays back many times over.

3Build it (Opus, with a team)

Once the plan's solid, switch to Opus, the deep thinker, and let your Chief of Staff run the build.

For a bigger job, your Chief of Staff brings in a team of smaller, faster helpers that take a piece each and work in parallel. You stay in the loop: watch, steer, approve as it goes.

4Get a second opinion (a Codex audit)

When it's nearly there, ask your Chief of Staff to write an audit prompt for Codex, a second AI from OpenAI, a fresh set of eyes. You only need an ordinary ChatGPT subscription for it.

Open the Codex desktop app, point it at the same folder, and paste in the prompt. Codex reviews the work for security, efficiency, and anything else worth improving, then hands back a report.

5Tie up the loose ends

Bring Codex's report back to your Chief of Staff and work through it together, fixing what's worth fixing.

Then go round again: build, audit, tidy, until it's done.

Vision first. Plan until it's right. Build. Get a second opinion. Tidy up. That loop builds anything.
