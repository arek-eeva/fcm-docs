Build your own Chief of Staff

Before we begin.

Hi Tamir. A little prep before our session means we spend the time building, not gathering. None of this needs tidying. Rough and real is exactly right, and the more of your actual business you bring, the faster yours comes to life.

What to bring

Drop it all in one folder. Screenshots, exports, PDFs, links, whatever's easiest.

  • Your brand

    Your logo, your colours and fonts if you know them, any brand or style guide, and a couple of words on how you like to sound. If you don't have a formal brand, just point me at your website or socials.

  • Your proposals

    One or two you've actually sent. They show me how you scope, structure, and price your work.

  • Your invoices

    A couple of recent ones plus your blank template or format, so your assistant can match how you bill.

  • Examples of your work

    A few things you're proud of: a good email, a post, a write-up, a testimonial, a piece of marketing. These teach your assistant your voice and your standard.

  • Your prices

    A rate card or a plain list of what you charge and what's included, so quotes and enquiries come out consistent.

Bring your ChatGPT across

If you've been working with ChatGPT, it already knows a lot about your business. Let's carry that over rather than starting cold. Paste the prompt below into ChatGPT and it will write you a clean handover brief you can bring along.

Paste this into ChatGPT 
You're helping me hand over to a new AI assistant that will run the business side of my practice. Using everything you know about me and my work from our chats and this project, write a clear, plain handover brief a new assistant could read to get up to speed.

Cover these, with headings:
1. About me and my business: who I am, what I do, where, and one line on what makes my approach distinctive.
2. What I offer and what it costs: my services or programmes, and pricing if you know it.
3. Who I serve: my clients or audience, and the problem I help with.
4. My voice: how I write and speak, the words and tone I use, with two short examples in my style.
5. What we've worked on together: the projects, drafts and tasks you've helped me with, and where each one is up to.
6. My recurring tasks: the things I do regularly that an assistant could take off my plate.
7. My preferences: how I like to work, and what I do and don't want from an assistant.
8. Anything else important you know about my business.

Write it as one document I can copy out in full.

Important: keep it to the business layer only. Do not include any client names, case details, health information, or anything private about the people I work with. If something can't be shared, leave it out and say so.

Then:

  1. Run it once in each of your ChatGPT Projects. Each project remembers different things, so each will give you a different brief.
  2. Run it once in a normal chat too, to catch anything in ChatGPT's general memory.
  3. Copy each result into a single document, or just bring the separate copies. Either is fine.
  4. Optional: if you'd like the raw history as well, you can export it from ChatGPT under Settings, Data controls, Export data. The summaries above are usually all we need.

One boundary

Your Chief of Staff handles the business layer: marketing, admin, scheduling, templates, content, and correspondence. It doesn't hold client records, session notes, or sensitive personal information.

As you gather samples and run that prompt, keep client-identifying detail out. If a proposal or an example names a client, swap in a placeholder or take that part out first. This protects you, your clients, and your registration.

That's everything. Bring what you've got to the session and we'll build the rest together. If you're unsure whether something is useful, bring it anyway.
